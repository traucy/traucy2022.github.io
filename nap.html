For your mind and the people in your life.

Intro

On the product design team at Daybreak, I worked on Notes About People to create a tool that provides not just a quiet introspection to our thoughts, but a self portrait of the relationships that surround them.

My Contribution

Designed the UI and experience for the Home, Books, and People feature. Prototyped mobile and desktop screens using Figma and After Effects. The whole process involved systems thinking, where the goal was to create an ecosystem of parts that connected the user to other people and artifacts.

How often do you interact with others in your day to day life?

Your 1:1s, introductions to new colleagues, catching up with friends, and collaborating with people are just a few things we do.

While there are many note taking tools out there, Notes About People was designed with a balanced disposition that invites you to organize your notes and relationships without extraneous elements getting in your way.

The only pages that you navigate to are your notes — everything else lives contextually to you through modals.

Manage People

The People modal allows you to keep reference points for the people in your life. From new introductions to your meetings with colleagues, you’re able to easily keep track of your notes and who’s part of it.

Track Tasks

The To Do modal lives contextually to where you are and allows you to track action items — both for yourself and others.

Stay Organized

For collaborative projects, create books to organize your notes and share them with people you know.

Books

Create a book where you're able to swiftly share a set of notes with people and the contacts that are mentioned within the notes.

Notes

Easily see who will be losing access and gaining it when moving your notes between spaces, and change the level of access people have within a book between viewer, editor and owner.
